# Transition Types & Prompting Guide

## Transition Prompt Structure

Every transition prompt should describe three things:

1. **START STATE** ‚Äî What the viewer sees in the first frame
2. **TRANSFORMATION** ‚Äî What happens during the transition (the journey)
3. **END STATE** ‚Äî What the viewer sees in the last frame

### Template:
```
[Describe the start frame in detail ‚Äî style, colors, composition].
[Describe the trigger moment ‚Äî what initiates the change].
[Describe the transformation ‚Äî what visually happens as styles morph].
[Describe the end state ‚Äî the new style fully realized].
```

### Always include:
- **Negative prompt:** What to avoid (realistic, text, UI, fast cuts, etc.)
- **Motion level:** Very low / Low / Medium / Medium-high / High
- **Duration suggestion:** Based on complexity (simple: 5-6s, complex: 8-10s)

## Transformation Types

### Organic Transformation
*One style naturally grows into another*

Best for: watercolor‚Üíanime, pencil‚Üíwatercolor, cartoon‚Üístorybook

Example trigger words: "bleeds into", "grows from", "emerges", "softens into", "color spreads"

### Disintegration / Reformation  
*Old style breaks apart, new style forms from the pieces*

Best for: any style‚Üípixel, any style‚Üíglitch, digital‚Üíorganic

Example trigger words: "fragments into", "pixels dissolve", "shatters and reforms", "deconstructs"

### Chemical / Alchemical
*Transformation through a catalytic moment*

Best for: realistic‚Üípsychedelic, cartoon‚Üíneon, any‚Üícosmic

Example trigger words: "ignites", "reacts", "transmutes", "crystallizes", "melts"

### Environmental Shift
*The world around the subject changes, pulling the subject with it*

Best for: any style pair where the background drives the mood

Example trigger words: "the background darkens", "light shifts", "the world transforms", "surroundings morph"

### Corruption / Purification
*Light-to-dark or dark-to-light moral shifts*

Best for: ‚Üíeldritch, ‚Üínoir, horror‚Üícartoon, dark‚Üílight

Example trigger words: "cracks appear", "darkness seeps", "tendrils emerge", "light returns", "shadows retreat"

### Digital Glitch
*Reality breaks ‚Äî scan lines, pixel corruption, chromatic aberration*

Best for: ‚Üípixel art, horror‚Üídigital, any dramatic cut

Example trigger words: "reality glitches", "scan lines tear", "the image corrupts", "static bursts"

## Music Video Transition Effects (applied in post-production)

| Effect | When to Use | Frames |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| **White Flash** | On musical hits, chorus entries, dramatic moments | 3-5 frames |
| **Crossfade** | Smooth musical transitions, verse-to-verse, gentle moments | 12-24 frames |
| **Glitch Cut** | Digital themes, reboot moments, genre shifts | 8-14 frames |
| **Hard Cut** | On strong downbeats, clean section changes | 1 frame |
| **Fade to Black** | Between acts, before dramatic reveals | 24-48 frames |

## Duration Guidelines

| Transition Complexity | Suggested Duration | Notes |
|----------------------|-------------------|-------|
| Simple style shift (cartoon‚Üípixel) | 5-6 seconds | Not much to show |
| Moderate transformation (pencil‚Üíwatercolor) | 8 seconds | Standard |
| Complex with story beat (anime‚Üíshroom) | 8-10 seconds | Needs time for narrative |
| Dramatic peak (LSD‚ÜíDMT) | 8 seconds | Intense, don't linger |
| Emotional (horror‚Üírecovery) | 10 seconds | Needs breathing room |
| Gentle ending (cartoon‚Üíwatercolor) | 10-12 seconds | Let it fade gracefully |
